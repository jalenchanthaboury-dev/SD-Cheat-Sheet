Charter Industries Extrusions LLC
Lindy Bonter, Head of HR  ·  Feb 26, 2026  ·  28 min
Closed Won
▼
They make the thin strips of material that wrap around the edges of furniture in hospitals, schools, hotels, and restaurants. If a piece of furniture has a clean finished edge, Charter probably made that material.
Closed revenue
$18,981.57
Contract
12 months
Product
1 recruiter seat, 3 job slots
AE
Jack Aiello
What I uncovered
Just acquired by private equity with aggressive growth targets already in motion. More revenue meant more sales reps, faster than their current process could support.
surfaced when I asked what was going on in the business prompting these hires
Only HR person at a 70 person company handling all recruiting herself across multiple platforms with zero automation.
surfaced when I asked who else was involved in the recruiting process
Running on pay per click postings with no easy apply, no pre-qualifiers, no ATS integration. Every application required a manual response.
surfaced when Lindy described her day to day frustrations unprompted
Busy season starts late spring through fall. New reps had to be hired and trained before that window or the revenue opportunity was gone for the year.
surfaced when I asked what the timeline looked like to fill these seats
Business impact and urgency
Missing the hiring window meant stagnant accounts sitting untouched. Customers who had not ordered in months with no one to follow up. Revenue on the table with a countdown clock.
Current reps spread thin. No capacity to protect or grow existing accounts during the highest revenue period of the year.
PE firm with growth mandates. Lindy's hiring performance was directly tied to whether the company hit its board targets.
"If we don't hire people in time, those accounts will just be on the back burner."
A PE-backed company entering its busiest season with stagnant accounts and no one to work them. I connected that thread clearly and it helped this deal close.
BANT
Budget
Asked directly. Board approves headcount and budget follows the role. I also surfaced that their PPC spend had been running 6 months with limited results, which built the ROI case for a fixed cost solution.
probed and applied
Authority
Lindy owns recruiting day to day. Board approves added roles. I confirmed the decision path and framed the next call accordingly.
Need
Qualified applicants, easy apply, ATS integration, pre-qualifiers, less manual work. All surfaced by Lindy unprompted.
Timeline
One month before busy season. Surfaced but left too general. Should have asked what one missed hire costs in revenue during peak season.
partially explored
What I would do differently
On timeline urgency
Should have quantified what one unfilled rep costs during peak season. If accounts do $1M in orders and peak is 60 to 90 days, that is a real number. I left it abstract.
On stagnant accounts
Lindy mentioned dormant accounts. I summarized instead of digging in. How many? What average order size? That thread could have made the deal feel like an obvious yes before the AE even got on a call.
This is the call that closed, and the budget conversation is why. The one call where I asked about ROI is the one that closed. Not a coincidence. Carrying that into every call going forward.

CN-Seamless Inc.
Dario Muller, Co-Founder  ·  Mar 3, 2026  ·  29 min
Qualified
▼
They make a 25-pound robot that steel workers carry to a job site, clamp onto metal, and control from an iPad to cut precise shapes automatically. A CNC machine that fits in a backpack.
What I uncovered
Dario co-founded this company and built the product. He is also personally screening 350 resumes because no one else can evaluate an engineering resume. The person who should be building the product is doing recruiting.
surfaced when I asked who was primarily handling screening
On Recruiter Lite for a three to four engineer hiring push. Dario called it "a downgrade." The tool was not built for this.
surfaced when Dario walked me through why he booked the call
$15M revenue target for 2026 tied directly to launching new products that need software engineers. The hiring timeline and the revenue target are the same deadline.
surfaced when I asked what was driving these hires and what revenue they were targeting
Fully bootstrapped and profitable. 300 units sold at $18K each. Every dollar spent on a tool has to justify itself.
surfaced organically as Dario described company growth
Business impact and urgency
"Last year we had a hard time releasing anything new." Frozen product development because two people could not maintain the existing product and build new things simultaneously.
New products are the path to $15M. Every month a hire is delayed is a month of product development that does not happen.
One hire per month in April, May, June. Missing one window pushes a product launch and delays revenue at $18K per unit.
"I don't want to spend 20 hours every other week looking through resumes."
A co-founder spending time on manual screening instead of building the product is a real business cost. I had the opening to quantify it and I did not take it.
BANT
Budget
Bootstrapped but profitable. I never asked what Recruiter Lite cost them, whether it delivered, or what they would put toward something that actually worked.
gap I own
Authority
Dario leads for engineering and tooling. Co-founders Josh and Sam not active in this push. Never asked if they would need to sign off on a tool purchase.
Need
Pre-filtered shortlists, pipeline stages, team visibility, proactive sourcing. All came up naturally.
Timeline
April, May, June. One per month. Front end already in interviews.
What I would do differently
On budget
Self-funded companies spend on things they can justify. I knew that going in and still skipped it. The AE walked in without a pricing anchor.
On the cost of a co-founder's time
Dario laughed off the time he spent on resumes. I let him. I should have slowed down and made it a real dollar amount.
On evaluation criteria
He said Recruiter Lite felt like a downgrade. Someone already burned by a LinkedIn product. I should have asked what would need to be true for him to feel confident this time.
Same gap as Rwazi. Strong discovery, strong vision match, left before the business case was fully built.

Rwazi Inc.
Tinashe Sibanda, Chief of Staff  ·  Mar 23, 2026  ·  34 min
Qualified
▼
Rwazi helps companies stop making gut feel decisions by giving them real time consumer data across 195 countries so they always know what to do next.
What I uncovered
27 open roles, 200 applicants each, 3 viable. Process drowning in noise with no way to filter for who actually mattered.
asked: which roles are most urgent and what does your process look like today?
Using Sales Navigator as a recruiting tool, a mismatch they had normalized. Gaps invisible until I surfaced it.
asked: how are you using LinkedIn today?
Inbound exploding but no one owned pricing or operations. Salespeople making pricing decisions on the fly.
asked: what's making these roles so critical right now?
Business impact and urgency
Finance unfilled means nobody owns pricing. Deals closing at wrong numbers. Compounds into a structural revenue problem fast.
Ops unfilled means daily delivery is unmanaged. Every week those roles sit open, customer commitments are at risk.
Investor conversations already uncomfortable. Board level pressure, not just an HR problem.
"It would make a big dent on our ARR."
Tinashe's exact words when I asked how much revenue they were leaving on the table. For a Series A company that is a board deck number, not a rounding error.
BANT
Budget
Not probed. Assumed Series A stage meant budget. That was lazy. Never asked what they had spent on tools or what solving this was worth.
gap I own
Authority
Tinashe ~70%. Joseph (co-founder) secondary. Confirmed engaged but never asked what would make him push back.
Need
Candidate quality, AI sourcing, filter accuracy, speed to hire. All surfaced organically.
Timeline
Immediate. 27 live roles. Revenue leaking weekly. Booked AE walkthrough same call.
What I would do differently
On budget
When Tinashe said "big dent on ARR" I should have asked what they would invest to fix it and what they had spent on tools before. Left the business case half built.
On authority
I assumed Joseph was aligned without earning that assumption. Never asked what would make him hesitate. A co-founder with 30% decision power can kill a deal.
I am catching these gaps myself now rather than waiting to be told. That is the progress. But awareness without change is just journaling.

Q4 focus areas
Focus 01
Activating urgency
Quantifying pain so it is impossible to ignore. Not just surfacing that a problem exists, but tying it to a real number so the prospect understands the cost of doing nothing. Revenue at risk, time being lost, a deadline with consequences.
Focus 02
Solution mapping
Taking a challenge articulated by a customer and mapping it to a specific solution we can provide, concisely. No feature dumping. Just a clear line from their problem to our answer.
Focus 03
Demo readiness
SDRs do not run demos. I am building toward it anyway. Studying how AEs structure their flow, what they anchor on, and how they connect product back to discovery. I want to be ready before I need to be.

Mentoring Ross LaBauex
